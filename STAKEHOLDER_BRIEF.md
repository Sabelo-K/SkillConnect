## Executive Summary

South Africa faces one of the world's worst unemployment crises — yet millions of skilled workers exist in every township and peri-urban community, ready to work. The gap is not a lack of skill. The gap is **visibility, trust, and connection**.

SkillConnect closes that gap. Our platform allows any community member to find a verified local plumber, electrician, carpenter, painter, tiler, builder, or welder within minutes — using nothing more than a smartphone. Workers earn more consistently. Clients hire with confidence. Money that previously left the community now circulates within it.

We operate on a **commission-only model** (6–12%, charged on completion only), meaning we only earn when local workers earn. We are currently in active pilot in Sweetwaters, Pietermaritzburg — and we are seeking municipal partnerships, NGO collaborations, and strategic investors to accelerate ward-by-ward expansion across KwaZulu-Natal.

## 1. The Problem: South Africa's Invisible Skilled Worker Crisis

### National unemployment — the scale of the challenge

| Indicator | Figure | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Overall unemployment rate | **32.7%** | StatsSA Q1 2026 |
| Youth unemployment (ages 15–34) | **46.1%** | StatsSA QLFS Q1 2025 |
| Youth unemployment (ages 15–24, narrow) | **~59.9%** | Statista / ILO 2025 |
| Youth with no prior work experience | **58.7%** of 4.8M unemployed youth | StatsSA |
| Informal sector share of workforce | **42%** (expanded definition) | StatsSA QLFS 2024 |

South Africa has one of the highest youth unemployment rates in the world. This is not a temporary fluctuation — youth unemployment has risen by 9.2 percentage points in a single decade (from 36.9% in Q1 2015 to 46.1% in Q1 2025).

### The artisan shortage paradox

South Africa simultaneously faces a **documented shortage of skilled artisans**. The DHET's *2024 National List of Occupations in High Demand* (published in the Government Gazette) explicitly lists plumbers, electricians, carpenters, welders, and related trades as scarce skills. Yet:

- **19% of artisans who pass the national trade test are unemployed**
- **Only 2% of qualified artisans are formally self-employed**
- Many more operate informally without any certificate

*(Source: DHET Artisan Tracer Study)*

The problem is not supply. It is the absence of a reliable, trusted channel connecting skilled workers to paying clients.

### Digital invisibility at scale

A formal mapping exercise found that **79% of informal businesses are not listed on any digital platform — not even Google Maps** (Acalytica). This means skilled workers who have spent years developing their craft are entirely invisible to potential clients searching online. Without digital presence, they rely entirely on word-of-mouth, which severely limits earning potential and geographic reach — even within their own community.

Barriers to digital visibility for informal artisans include:
- No formal business registration
- Cash-only operations with no digital transaction history
- No marketing tools or access
- Limited device access and data affordability
- Digital literacy gaps
- Trust and safety concerns about online platforms

*(Source: Acalytica / BizCommunity)*

### The township economy and economic leakage

South Africa's township economy is estimated at **R900 billion to R1 trillion annually** (Daily Investor, 2024). Yet much of this value leaks out because residents cannot access reliable local skilled labour for home improvements, repairs, and construction — and instead hire from outside the ward, or defer work indefinitely.

Key data points:
- **51% of township residents shop daily at their local spaza** — intent to buy local exists
- **60% of pensioners spend more than half their income within the township** when local options are available
- **Less than 9% of township businesses access bank loans** — they cannot grow to absorb local demand
- The informal township FMCG market grew at **6.7% per year** between 2018–2023, outpacing much of the formal economy

*(Sources: Imali Matters / SME South Africa / Arabian Post)*

Every job captured by an outsider is money that does not stay local. SkillConnect is a mechanism for **economic retention**.

## 2. Our Solution

SkillConnect is a **locality-first digital labour marketplace** designed for exactly this context.

### Core principles

| Principle | What it means in practice |
|---|---|
| **Locality-first matching** | Workers from the client's own ward are always surfaced first. A plumber in Ward 4 gets the Ward 4 job first. Money stays local. |
| **Worker verification** | Every worker is vetted: ID, proof of local residence, and portfolio photos of past work. No formal certificate required — skill is demonstrated. |
| **Transparent ratings** | Real community reviews after every job. Clients know exactly who they are letting into their home before they arrive. |
| **Mobile-first design** | Works on any smartphone without an app download. Built for the 74.7% of South Africans who access the internet via mobile device. *(DataReportal 2024)* |
| **POPIA compliance** | Full Privacy Policy and data consent flows built into every registration. Worker and client data is handled in line with the Protection of Personal Information Act. |

### Trades currently on the platform

Plumber · Electrician · Carpenter · Painter · Tiler · Builder · Welder · General Handyman

## 3. How It Works

### For clients
1. **Tell us what you need** — select the trade and describe the job. No long forms.
2. **Get matched instantly** — our locality-first algorithm finds the best available worker in your ward.
3. **Review and confirm** — view the worker's verified profile, photos of past work, and community ratings.
4. **Get it done** — the worker comes to you. Pay directly. Leave an honest review.

### For workers
1. **Register your skills** — share your trade, location, and photos of past work. No formal certificate required.
2. **Get verified** — our team reviews and approves your profile within 48 hours.
3. **Receive job requests** — clients in your area find you first. You choose which jobs to accept.
4. **Get paid and grow** — clients pay you directly. A small commission (6–12%) is deducted only when the job is marked complete.

## 4. Business Model

| Component | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Free for workers** | No upfront registration costs. Workers join, get verified, and receive job requests at no charge. |
| **Free for clients** | Job requests, matching, and communication are free. |
| **6–12% commission on completion** | SkillConnect earns only when a job is successfully completed. |
| **No hidden fees** | Transparent at every step. Workers keep the majority of what they earn. |

**Revenue alignment note:** Our commercial incentives are identical to the community's interests. We grow by helping workers earn more, not by charging them for access or taking upfront fees they cannot afford.

### Policy tailwind: COGTA's National LED Framework 2018–2028

South Africa's national Local Economic Development Framework explicitly tasks municipalities with driving LED — and identifies townships as priority zones. The *OECD Economic Survey of South Africa 2025* explicitly recommends reducing digital and financial barriers for informal businesses, noting that "standards and licences are often unaffordable or overly complex."

SkillConnect is not working against policy — it is the **operational implementation** of what these frameworks call for, built and deployed at ground level without requiring a municipal budget.

In **KwaZulu-Natal specifically**, the uMgungundlovu District Municipality (which covers Pietermaritzburg and surrounding areas including Sweetwaters) has active LED programming — creating a direct pathway for municipal partnership.

## 6. Why SkillConnect?

### Community-embedded, not extractive
Unlike national gig platforms that extract commission and redirect value to distant shareholders, SkillConnect is built by and for the Sweetwaters community. The founding team lives and works in the area it serves. Revenue earned stays within the KZN economy.

### Aligned with national LED policy
COGTA's National LED Framework 2018–2028 tasks municipalities with driving local economic development. SkillConnect operationalises this at ground level — no government budget required, no procurement process needed to begin.

### Low barrier to entry by design
No formal certificate is needed to register. Workers upload photos of their work and a local reference. This is critical: it includes the 19% of certificated artisans who are unemployed, and the far larger population of skilled workers who have never had access to formal certification.

### POPIA compliant from day one
Full data consent flows, a published Privacy Policy, and transparent data handling are built into every interaction — not added as an afterthought. Workers and clients know exactly what data is collected and why.

### Verification builds community trust
Every worker goes through a manual approval process before appearing publicly. This is a deliberate design choice over automated systems — it ensures that clients in Sweetwaters can trust the person they are inviting into their home, and that workers on the platform are genuinely part of the community.
